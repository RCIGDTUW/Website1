Is Your Sanitary Napkin Harmful?

Most women are not aware that even sanitary pads are made of elements that are non-biodegradable and could pose a health risk, said doctors. “These napkins are instrumental in severely damaging the reproductive system and can result in a serious hormonal imbalance,“ they said. Physician and medical officer, Thane Municipal Corporation (TMC), Dr Khushbu Tavdi, said, “Women must frequently change their sanitary pads within regular intervals of six hours failing which there are chances that they might get infected with vaginal infections such as vulvovaginitis, the inflammation of the vulva and vagina. Prolonged use of these products can also give rise to rashes and skin diseases. Apart from this, there is also a high possibility of developing cervical and uterine cancers in the later stages of life.“

Experts said that since no one talks openly about `female problems' there is still a large gap to be filled when it comes to menstrual hygiene and awareness. Nishant Bangera, a citybased social activist, who conducts lectures and training on sustainable menstrual methods, said, “Sustainable menstruation means environment friendly, non-hazardous menstruation through methods which do not damage the environment and are practical and economical. The usage of sanitary napkins is not only detrimental to women's health, but its system of disposal has harmful environmental effects.It is said that one sanitary napkin is equivalent to four plastic bags. To eliminate this problem altogether, we must try to find alternate solutions to replace sanitary napkins.“
